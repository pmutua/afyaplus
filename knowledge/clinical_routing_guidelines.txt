AfyaPlus Health — Clinical Routing Guidelines
(Synthetic reference document for capstone RAG grounding. Not real
clinical guidance. Does not replace the separate Triage Engine component.)

1. Routing Destinations

Every patient message is routed to exactly one of three destinations:

- Emergency Medical Call Team: for cases with an immediate, life-threatening
  danger sign. This routing bypasses all insurance pre-authorization checks
  under the Emergency Care Guarantee.
- Urgent Nurse Callback: for cases that are concerning but not immediately
  life-threatening, needing clinical judgment within a few hours.
- General Queue: for routine, non-urgent cases that can wait for a standard
  appointment slot.

2. Pregnancy Danger Signs

Route to Emergency Medical Call Team if a pregnant patient reports any of:
severe or persistent headache combined with sudden swelling of the hands,
face, or feet; heavy vaginal bleeding; reduced or absent fetal movement past
28 weeks; or fluid leaking before 37 weeks. A single mild symptom in
isolation (e.g. ordinary swelling late in pregnancy with no headache) may be
routed to Urgent Nurse Callback instead.

3. Pediatric Danger Signs

Route to Emergency Medical Call Team if a child presents with any of:
fever combined with lethargy, repeated vomiting, or inability to drink
fluids; breathing difficulty; seizure activity; or a rash that does not fade
under pressure. A child with fever alone, active and drinking normally, may
be routed to Urgent Nurse Callback or General Queue depending on how many
days the fever has persisted.

4. Cardiac and Respiratory Danger Signs

Route to Emergency Medical Call Team for chest pain combined with shortness
of breath, chest pain radiating to the arm or jaw, sudden severe breathing
difficulty, or blue discoloration of the lips or fingertips. Mild, chronic,
exertion-only shortness of breath in a patient with a known stable condition
may be routed to Urgent Nurse Callback.

5. Bleeding and Trauma

Route to Emergency Medical Call Team for uncontrolled or heavy bleeding,
suspected fracture with visible deformity, or loss of consciousness
following any injury. Minor cuts, bruises, and stable old injuries are
routed to General Queue.

6. Mental Health Crisis

Route to Emergency Medical Call Team for any explicit statement of intent
to self-harm or harm others, or a described suicide plan. General mental
health concerns without an immediate safety risk (e.g. ongoing low mood,
sleep difficulty) are routed to Urgent Nurse Callback for a same-day
clinical check-in.

7. Chronic Disease Follow-Up

Routine follow-up messages for previously diagnosed, currently stable
chronic conditions — such as a diabetes patient requesting a repeat
prescription, or a hypertension patient reporting normal home blood
pressure readings — are routed to General Queue. A chronic disease patient
reporting a new, severe, or worsening symptom outside their normal pattern
should instead be evaluated under the relevant danger-sign category above.

8. Interaction With Insurance Verification

Clinical routing decisions are made independently of insurance status. A
case classified as Emergency Medical Call Team is always escalated
immediately, even if member insurance verification has not yet completed;
insurance verification proceeds in parallel, never blocking emergency
routing. For Urgent Nurse Callback and General Queue cases, insurance
verification should complete before the appointment is scheduled, so the
correct coverage tier and any pre-authorization requirements are known in
advance.

9. When Uncertain

If a case does not clearly match any danger-sign category above but some
uncertainty remains about severity, prefer the higher-urgency routing
destination over the lower one. Under-routing a case that later escalates
is treated as a more serious failure than a low-urgency case receiving a
faster response than strictly required.
