1. Plan Tiers and Coverage

AfyaPlus Health offers four member plan tiers: Bronze, Silver, Gold, and
Platinum.

- Bronze covers 60% of outpatient consultation costs and 70% of inpatient
  costs, with an annual outpatient limit of KES 50,000.
- Silver covers 75% of outpatient costs and 80% of inpatient costs, with an
  annual outpatient limit of KES 150,000.
- Gold covers 85% of outpatient costs and 90% of inpatient costs, with an
  annual outpatient limit of KES 400,000.
- Platinum covers 100% of outpatient and inpatient costs with no annual
  outpatient limit.

All four tiers cover emergency care at 100%, regardless of pre-authorization
status, consistent with AfyaPlus's Emergency Care Guarantee.

2. Member Identification and Verification

Every AfyaPlus member is issued a unique member ID in the format AP-######
(the letters "AP", a hyphen, then six digits). Before any claim, referral, or
insurance verification is processed, staff must confirm:

- The member ID matches an active AfyaPlus account.
- The plan status is "active", not "lapsed" or "suspended". A lapsed plan
  has no coverage until the next premium payment clears.
- The requested service is covered under the member's current tier.

Member identity may be confirmed using the member ID together with either
the phone number or email address on file for that account. A verification
request is only considered complete once both the member ID and at least one
contact identifier match AfyaPlus records.

3. Waiting Periods

New members are subject to the following waiting periods before coverage
begins for non-emergency services:

- General outpatient consultations: no waiting period.
- Specialist referrals: 30-day waiting period from enrollment date.
- Elective surgery and non-emergency inpatient procedures: 90-day waiting
  period from enrollment date.
- Maternity coverage: 180-day waiting period from enrollment date.

Waiting periods do not apply to emergency care under any circumstances.

4. Pre-Authorization Requirements

The following services require pre-authorization from AfyaPlus before the
service is rendered, except when the presenting case is a clinical
emergency:

- Any elective surgery.
- MRI and CT imaging.
- Specialist referrals outside of the member's assigned primary care
  provider network.
- Inpatient admissions expected to exceed 3 days.

Pre-authorization requests are typically resolved within 2 business days.
Emergency admissions and emergency imaging never require pre-authorization;
they are verified retroactively within 5 business days of the emergency
encounter.

5. Claim Submission Rules

Claims must be submitted within 90 days of the date of service. Claims
submitted after this window are denied unless the delay was caused by a
documented AfyaPlus system outage. Each claim must reference:

- The member ID.
- The date and nature of service.
- The treating provider's registration number.

6. Dependents Coverage

A primary member may add a spouse and up to four dependent children under
the age of 21 to their plan. Dependents share the primary member's plan tier
and annual outpatient limit; they do not receive a separate limit.

7. Exclusions

AfyaPlus plans at all tiers exclude coverage for cosmetic procedures not
medically necessary, experimental treatments not approved by the Kenya
Ministry of Health, and injuries sustained during participation in
professional contact sports. These exclusions do not apply to emergency
stabilization care, which is always covered regardless of the underlying
cause of injury.

8. Regulatory Framework

AfyaPlus Health operates as a private complementary health insurer licensed
and supervised by the Insurance Regulatory Authority (IRA) of Kenya under
the Insurance Act (Cap. 487). AfyaPlus plans are complementary cover: they
sit alongside, and do not replace, the mandatory Social Health Insurance
Fund (SHIF) administered by the Social Health Authority (SHA), Kenya's
national social health insurance scheme established under the Social Health
Insurance Act, 2023. Every AfyaPlus member is expected to maintain active
SHA registration; AfyaPlus verification staff may record a member's SHA
number alongside their AP-###### member ID for coordination-of-benefits
purposes, but SHA registration status itself does not gate AfyaPlus
verification or claims processing.

9. Coordination of Benefits with SHA/SHIF

Where a service is covered under both the member's SHIF benefit package and
their AfyaPlus plan tier, AfyaPlus pays only the balance remaining after the
SHIF-covered portion, up to the member's AfyaPlus tier limit — members are
never reimbursed twice for the same billed service. Where a service is
outside the SHIF benefit package entirely (for example, cover above SHIF's
inpatient bed-day limits, or a benefit SHIF excludes), AfyaPlus applies its
normal tier percentage to the full billed amount. Verification staff must
request the SHA claim reference number (if any) before finalizing an
AfyaPlus claim for a service that could plausibly be SHIF-covered.

10. Data Protection During Verification

Member verification and claims processing involve personal and health data
protected under Kenya's Data Protection Act, 2019, and its implementing
Data Protection Policy. Verification staff must:

- Collect and process only the data fields necessary for the verification
  or claim at hand (member ID plus one contact identifier, per Section 2) —
  never request additional personal data "for the file" without a specific,
  documented purpose.
- Treat phone numbers, email addresses, member IDs, and any clinical detail
  disclosed during verification as confidential; these must never be
  shared outside the verification and claims workflow without the member's
  explicit consent or a valid legal basis.
- Honor a member's data-subject rights under the Act, including the right
  to access, correct, or request deletion of their AfyaPlus records,
  subject to AfyaPlus's regulatory retention obligations under the
  Insurance Act.
- Escalate any suspected data breach involving member records to AfyaPlus's
  designated data protection contact immediately, consistent with the
  Data Protection Act's breach-notification requirements.

This system's own privacy-masking middleware enforces the same principle in
software: phone numbers, emails, and member IDs are masked before reaching
any AI model, and are restored only in the final response shown to the
requesting user.

11. Fraud Prevention and Verification Integrity

Verification staff must flag, rather than silently approve or silently
deny, any of the following for human review: a member ID presented with
contact details that do not match AfyaPlus records; repeated verification
attempts for the same member ID from different, unrelated contact details
within a short period; or a claim whose service date precedes the member's
enrollment date. No claim is auto-denied for suspected fraud without a
documented human review — this system routes and informs, it does not make
final fraud determinations.

12. Complaints and Dispute Resolution

A member who disputes a verification outcome, claim decision, or waiting
period calculation may first escalate to AfyaPlus's internal complaints
desk, which must acknowledge the complaint within 2 business days and
resolve or provide a substantive response within 14 business days. If
unresolved, the member may escalate to the IRA's Insurance Complaints and
Compensation Fund process. Complaints alleging mishandling of personal data
specifically may instead, or additionally, be raised with the Office of the
Data Protection Commissioner (ODPC).

13. Plan Renewal and Waiting-Period Portability

AfyaPlus plans renew annually on the member's original enrollment
anniversary. A member who upgrades tier (e.g. Silver to Gold) mid-term does
not restart the waiting periods in Section 3 for services their prior tier
already covered; only the incremental coverage newly available at the
higher tier is subject to a fresh 90-day waiting period. A member who
lapses and later re-enrolls is treated as a new member for waiting-period
purposes, per Section 3.
